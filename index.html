Course Description

The course will cover second semester calculus, including: techniques of integration, improper integrals, Taylor expansions and error estimations , elementary first order differential equations, sequences and series, introduction to vectors.

Student Resources

If you are concerned about your background for Math 222, you may want to review materials from Math 221. Some old Math 221 exams can be found in this link. Reviewing differentiation and integration will be a good start for the semester. Understanding lectures and the TA discussions is important for you to learn the materials. You cannot learn math by watching how other people are doing. Do not fall behind on weekly homework assignments. Show work and justifications in homework. If you need extra help, don't hesitate to contact me. Besides office hours held by me or Professor Gong, there are other resources worth looking at:

  • MathLab - Free drop-in tutoring is available in B227 Van Vleck, Monday through Thursday from 3:30 AM - 8:30 PM and Sunday 3:30 AM - 6:50 PM.
  • Math Tutorial Program - Free small group tutoring is offered to students who may be failing, students who have not had a math course in several years, or students who are retaking the course. A significant time commitment is required. Any student can apply to the program, but after the first four weeks of the semester, a referral from an instructor is required. Students may apply in room 320 Van Vleck. If you feel like you may need a referral, you can talk to me or Professor Gong about it.
  • Tutoring in the University Residence Halls - Free drop-in math tutoring is available at various residence halls.
  • GUTS - Greater University Tutoring Service offers free small group, individual, and drop-in tutoring at various locations around campus. It is staffed mostly by student volunteers. Stop by their office (333 East Campus Mall, Rm. 4413) to sign up for a tutor or try drop-in tutoring.
  • Private Tutors - A list of tutors is available at the link. Feel free to contact them directly, or fill out this online form to have an email sent on your behalf to all of them.

Academic Integrity

I expect you to finish and hand in work on time. Being late to lectures or discussions, leaving early, using electronic devices which are not related to learning in this class, are disrespectful and disruptive to your instructors and your fellow students.

You are expected to hold the academic integrity of this University. You are allowed to work in groups when you discuss homework. However, you must write your own solutions. On quizzes and exams, academic dishonesty includes (but is not limited to) looking at another student’s paper and using references or devices which are not permitted. We treat all incidents of academic dishonesty very seriously. For instance, the consequences for cheating on an exam may result in automatically failing the course. Such an incident will be reported to the Dean of Students Office. For details about academic integrity, visit the web page in Dean of Students Office.
